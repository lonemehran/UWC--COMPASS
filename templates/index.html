What is UWC?

United World Colleges is a global education movement with 18 schools and colleges across four continents. Founded in 1962, UWC brings together young people from over 155 countries with the mission of making education a force to unite people, nations, and cultures for peace and a sustainable future.

Students study the International Baccalaureate Diploma Programme (IBDP), a respected curriculum that prepares them for university and fosters community support, leadership, teamwork, and creativity. More than 80% of UWC students receive financial support thanks to generous donors and partners.

An Education Like No Other

IB Diploma Programme

All UWC students study the internationally recognized IBDP. This rigorous academic program prepares students for university worldwide and fosters critical thinking, intercultural understanding, and respect.

Davis UWC Scholars

UWC graduates can access the Davis UWC Scholars program, which has provided funding for over 14,700 students in U.S. universities, including Ivy League colleges.

Lifelong Impact

Scholarship and alumni initiatives drive change in climate, justice, human rights and more. The alumni network spans 85,000+ graduates across the globe.
